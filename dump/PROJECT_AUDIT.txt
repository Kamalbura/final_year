PROJECT AUDIT & IMPROVEMENT PLAN (Draft)
Date: 2025-11-23

1. CURRENT OBJECTIVE
Multivariate 15-minute air-quality forecasting (pm2_5, pm10, temperature, humidity). Recommended horizon: 16 steps (next 4 hours) for proactive alerts.

2. EXISTING ASSETS
Raw: feeds.csv
Cleaning script: complete_phase1_2.py (rename, datetime index, outlier thresholding, rolling mean fill, 15T resample -> feeds_cleaned_15T.csv)
Exploration: run_phase1_2.py + phase1_2_air_quality.ipynb
Feature Engineering / High Frequency: phase4_* notebooks
Model Training (LSTM/CNN/TCN/BiLSTM): phase6_* notebooks; model artifacts (*.pth)
Results: results.csv (metrics snapshot)

3. KEY ISSUES
- Reproducibility: No centralized seeds, no environment lock, ad-hoc notebook code.
- Data Leakage Risk: Aggressive rolling mean fill across entire series before splits.
- Missing Normalization: No scaling before deep models (potential gradient instability).
- Outlier Handling: Hard thresholds only; need robust winsorization or IQR-based capping.
- Split Strategy: Train/test not clearly codified; lack of validation and rolling-origin evaluation.
- Metrics: Limited to RMSE, MAE, R2; missing MASE, CRPS, interval coverage, error distribution.
- Baselines: No classical statistical (ARIMA/Prophet), persistence, or simple MLP baseline.
- Uncertainty: Deterministic point forecasts only; no quantile prediction or probabilistic intervals.
- Interpretability: No feature attributions (attention, SHAP) or temporal importance analysis.
- Configuration: Parameters scattered in notebooks; no unified config file.

4. TARGET PIPELINE (TO BUILD)
Raw -> Preprocess (winsorize, minimal forward/back fill, train-only scaling) -> Windowed dataset (input_length, forecast_horizon=16) -> Baselines (Persistence, ARIMA/Prophet, TCN, BiLSTM, SimpleMLP) -> Advanced Model (Temporal Fusion Transformer) with quantile outputs & MC dropout -> Evaluation (metrics, calibration, coverage, residual plots) -> Documentation & model card.

6. RESEARCH PAPER COMPARISON (Condensed)
- TCN Time-Series Forecasting: Highlights dilated causal convolutions; our TCN baseline to include proper weight init & residual blocks.
- Temporal Fusion Transformer (Lim et al. 2020): Multi-horizon, quantile loss, interpretable variable selection. We will adopt core ideas (static & time-varying features, gating, multi-head attention). Initial phase: simplified PyTorch implementation with quantile outputs.
- Informer / Efficient Attention for Long Sequences: Consider later if horizon extends beyond 16 steps.
- Probabilistic Forecasting (DeepAR / Quantile Regression): Justifies quantile loss + coverage metrics.
- Air Quality Spatiotemporal Papers (e.g., ST-LSTM variants): Future extension to include spatial sensors if available.

7. DATA HANDLING PLAN
- Outliers: Compute per-sensor IQR; cap outside [Q1 - 1.5*IQR, Q3 + 1.5*IQR] or explicit domain thresholds; log modifications.
- Missing: Limit interpolation within small gaps (<=3 consecutive); avoid using future info (perform after train/val/test split when possible for inference-like fill on validation/test).
- Scaling: Fit StandardScaler (or RobustScaler) on train only; persist scaler to disk.

8. SPLIT STRATEGY
- Chronological: Train (earliest 70%), Validation (next 15%), Test (final 15%).
- Rolling-origin CV (optional): Expand training window; evaluate on successive validation slices.

9. METRICS & REPORTING
- Point: RMSE, MAE, R2, MASE.
- Probabilistic: Pinball loss (quantiles), CRPS (approx), coverage of 80% interval.
- Diagnostics: Autocorrelation of residuals, histogram, Q-Q plot.

10. UNCERTAINTY APPROACH
- Quantile Regression (q=[0.1,0.5,0.9]) + MC dropout (N=30 samples) for predictive interval spread; compute empirical coverage.

11. INTERPRETABILITY
- TFT variable selection weights; attention on decoder steps.
- SHAP (Kernel/Deep) on validation subset for baseline models.
